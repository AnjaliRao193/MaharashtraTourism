About Raigad

Raigad Fort, perched at 2,700 feet in the Sahyadri range, is a symbol of Maratha pride and history. Once the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's empire, it witnessed his coronation in 1674. Known as the "Gibraltar of the East," the fort boasts robust architecture, stunning views, and landmarks like Takmak Tok and Hirakani Buruj. Accessible by a scenic trek or ropeway, it offers a blend of adventure and historical exploration. Raigad stands as a timeless testament to Shivaji Maharaj's vision and valor.
